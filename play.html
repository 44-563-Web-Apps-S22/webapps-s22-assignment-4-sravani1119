Tartuffe

ACT I

SCENE I

Madame Pernelle and her servant-- Flipote, Elmire, Mariane, Dorine, Damis, Cleante
PERNELLE
Let's go, Flipote, let's go. I hate this place.
Elmire.
I can't keep up, you rush at such a pace.
PERNELLE
[He begins to leave.]

Peace, my dear, peace; come no farther. I don't wish to cause you any bother.

ELMIRE

What duty demands, I insist on giving. But, mother, what has caused your hasty leaving?

PERNELLE

I just can't stand the way your household runs . . . And no one cares what I wish to have done. Oh, yes, I leave your household quite dissatisfied For all my wise advice has been defied . . . And nobody respects me, and everybody shouts, And truly this is a home for the king of louts! Dorine. If . . .

PERNELLE

You, my dearie, are a bold lassy, A little brazen and very sassy, You butt into everything to speak your mind. Damis.
